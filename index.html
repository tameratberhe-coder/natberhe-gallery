Biography

The Artist

Nat Berhe (born 1991, San Jose, California) is an American painter working in Southern California. Through bold, expressive oil paintings, he explores themes of identity, memory, and transformation — building meaning through successive layers of pigment and gesture. His abstract-figurative works feature ghostlike, distorted human forms rendered in vivid, clashing colors that pulse with raw emotional energy.

Before devoting himself to painting full-time, Berhe forged a career as a professional football player. At San Diego State University, he established himself as one of the most dynamic safeties in the Mountain West Conference. In 2014, he made history as the first Eritrean-American drafted into the National Football League, selected by the New York Giants. Over six seasons between the Giants and the Pittsburgh Steelers, he brought the same intensity and discipline that now defines his studio practice.

Berhe’s paintings draw from the vocabulary of post-war abstract expressionism — the gestural freedom of de Kooning, the color-field gravity of Rothko, the primal urgency of Basquiat. Yet his voice is entirely his own: each canvas is a battleground where the figurative and the abstract wage a quiet war, leaving behind spectral traces of the human form.

In 2025, Berhe’s work was the subject of Colorstraction: A Personal Perspective at the Sasse Museum of Art, a landmark exhibition that affirmed his place in the contemporary art landscape. That same momentum carries into 2026 with the launch of Nat Berhe Gallery, bringing his originals and limited-edition prints directly to collectors worldwide.
